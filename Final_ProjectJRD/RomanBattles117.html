Defeat of the Bastarnae.

Turning back the Scythians

  • Date: Spring, 29 BCE.
  • Location: Thrace/Moesia.
  • Participents: Romans: 4th Scythica, 5th Macedonica, & maybe 10th Fretensis Legions with unknown auxiliaries under command of Marcus Licinus Crassus.
    Scythians: Bastarnae tribe under the command of King Deldo.
  • Numbers: Romans: 10,000 - 15,000 legionaries and an unknown number of auxiliaries.
    Bastarnae: the whole tribe, numbers unknown.
  • Outcome: Roman Victory; destruction of the Bastarnae.

Second Cantabrian War

Treachery and Dishonor in the Cantabrian mountains

  • Date: 19 BCE.
  • Location: Cantabria, Hispania
  • Participents: Romans: 1st Germanica, 2nd Augusta, 4th Macedonica, 5th Alaudae, 6th Victrix, 9th Hispana, 10th Gemina, 20th Valeria, auxiliaries; Ala II Gallorum, Cohors II Gallorum, Ala II Thracum Victrix Civium Romanorum, Cohors IV Thracum Aequitata, Ala Parthorum Ala Augusta under the command of Marcus Agrippa.
    Cantabrians: escaped slaves and all males of fighting age, unknown leaders.
  • Numbers: Romans: 70,000 - 80,000 total troops.
    Cantabrians: 50,000 estimated.
  • Outcome: Roman victory; all Cantabrian males age 17-46 executed, disarmed the population and force migrated the Cantabrians out of the mountains to be distributed them around Iberia.

The Pannonian War

Four grueling years

  • Date: 6-9 CE.
  • Location: Pannonia and Dalmatia.
  • Participents: Romans: 7th Claudia, 8th Augusta, 9th Hispana, 11th Claudia, 13th Gemina, 14th Gemina, 15th Apollinaris, 20th Valeria, 21st Rapax Legions, Evocati militian men, and unknown auxiliaries under the various commands of Tiberius,Messallinus, Caecina Severus, Germanicus, Marcus Plautius Silvanus, Marcus Aemilius Lepidus with help from the Odrysians under the command of Rhoemetalces I, their king.
    The Daesitiates, Breuci, Dalmatae, Andizetes, Pannonians, Pirustae, Liburnians, Iapydes tribes under the triple command of Bato the Daesitiate, Bato the Breucian, and Pinnes.
  • Numbers: Romans: 100,000+ men.
    Dalmatians and Pannonians: 200,000+ men and 9,000+ cavalry.
  • Outcome: Roman victory; Pannonia and Dalmatia brought back into the Roman fold, continued to be an important recruiting ground for legionaries and auxiliaries.

Battle of Idistavisus

Arminius decisively defeated.

  • Date: Summer, 16 CE
  • Location: Idistavisus, on the East side of the Weser river, Germania.
  • Participents: Romans: 1st Germanica, 2nd Augusta, 5ht Alaudae, 13th Gemina, 14th Gemina, 16th Gallica, 20th Valeria, 21st Rapax Legions, two cohorts of Praetorian Guard, and auxiliaries from Gaul, Raetia, Batavia, Spain, Syria plus men from allied German tribes under the command of Germanicus Julius Caesar.
    Germans: Cherusci, Chatti, Marsi, Fosi, Usipetes, Tubantes, Bructeri, Cauchi, Angrivarii, Tencteri, Mattiaci, Langobardi, and Ampsivarii tribes under the command of Arminius.
  • Numbers: Romans: 74,000; 28,000 legionaries, 2,000 Praetorians, 30,000 auxiliaries, 6,000 cavalry, 2,000 horse archers, and 6,000 German allies.
    Germans: 50,000+
  • Outcome: Roman victory; Arminius loses face with the tribes, Germanicus can press his advantage.

First Battle of Bedriacum

The first battle of a civil war

  • Date: April 14th, 69 CE
  • Location: Bedriacum, North of the Po River, Italia.
  • Participents: Romans under Vitellius: Cohorts from the 1st Germanica, 4th Macedonica, 5th Alaudae, and 15th Primigeneia Legions, 21st Rapax and 1st Italica legions, an equal number of auxiliaries commanded by Aulus Vitellius Germanicus, Aulus Caecina Alienus, and Fabius Valens.
    Romans under Otho: Cohorts from the Praetorian guard, 13th Gemina, and 14th Gemina, 1st Adiutrix Legion, Evocati militia, likely unknown auxiliaries, and, reportedly, a complement of gladiators commanded by Marcus Salvius Otho, Gaius Suetonius Paulinus, and Lucius Salvius Otho Titianus.
  • Numbers: Romans under Vitellius: 70,000-75,000.
    Romans under Otho: 40,000 estimated.
  • Outcome: Victory for Vitellius; Otho commits suicde and Vitellius becomes emperor.

Seige og Jerusalem

Titus takes back the holy city.

  • Date: February - 8th September, 70 CE.
  • Location: Jerusalem, Judea.
  • Participents: Romans: Cohorts from the 18th and 3rd Cyrenaica Legions, 5th Macedonica, 15th Apollinaris, 10th Fretensis, 12th Fulminata Legions, unknown auxiliaries,and allied troops from Chalcis, Commagene, Emesa, and Arabia commanded by Titus Flavius Vespasianus son of Vespasian.
